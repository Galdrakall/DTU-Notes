## Course overview
- Formal languages, running times(polynomial and non polynomial)
- Randomized algorithms: Definitions and complexity classes
- Decision vs. optimization problems
- **NP**-completeness: proving **NP** and **NP** completeness
- Using randomization to solve problems exactly, approximately or heruistically
## Exam
- Seven assignments
- All aids, own computer with lecture notes, excercises etc.
## Structure of Exam
1. Design of a language (Q1)
2. Conversion decision to optimization (Q2)
3. Proving a problem in NP (Q3)
4. Proving a problem is NP complete (Q4)
5. Analysis of randomized (approximation) algorithms (Q5&Q6)
6. Multiple choice (Q10), 10 questions
### What to go over
- All assignments, mandatory and optional
